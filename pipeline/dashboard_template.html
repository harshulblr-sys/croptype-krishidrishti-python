Advisory ribbon shows the fused level per dekad. VCI is a single-season percentile against same-crop neighbours (no multi-year climatology available); soil-moisture proxy is Sentinel-1 VV scaled between the field's seasonal dry and wet references. The two Ks lines answer different questions: the FAO-56 line is the physics water balance's estimate of how stressed this crop would be on rain alone (the advisory's driver); the LSTM line is an AI estimate of the same quantity made purely from what the satellite sees, for use where weather data is missing. When the satellite line sits well above the water-balance line, the field is likely being irrigated — the crop looks healthier than rain alone could explain.
Advisory fuses a stage-aware FAO-56 crop-water balance (weather-driven demand, effective rainfall, root-zone depletion vs. the crop's readily-available-water trigger) with observed spectral stress (NDVI anomaly, VCI, NDMI, Sentinel-1 soil moisture). Crop identity per field comes from the project's 8-class classifier; growth-stage timing is anchored on each field's own NDVI green-up sowing date. The advisory level is set by fixed, auditable if-then rules, not a learned model: “Irrigation advised” fires when the root-zone bucket reaches the crop's readily-available-water trigger in a water-sensitive stage, or when moderate spectral stress coincides with a dry Sentinel-1 soil signal or Ks < 0.85; “Severe” when Ks < 0.85 and strong spectral stress coincide in a sensitive stage; “Watch” at 80% of the trigger depletion or mild spectral stress. The LSTM runs alongside as a satellite-only stress estimator for weather-sparse operation. All detection steps are causal (no future dekads used), so the pipeline can run mid-season.
